Does Climate Change Impact Stock Market Volatility?

The VIX is a measure of volatility for the S&P 500 stock index. I used the yfinance module in Python to retrieve VIX data via the Yahoo Finance API. SEE CODE.

I scraped hurricane data using PANDAS in python to investigate short term effects on volatility. SEE code.

I created a monthly moving average filter using python to align vix data and the hurricanes data. I also created a 14 point trailing moving average to account for weather forecasting expectations of hurricanes. The data shows that 13 out 19 times, VIX is higher than the monthly MA and the trailing MA of volatility.

despite pledges made at the climate summit cop26, the world is still heading for between 2.4 to 2.9 degrees celcius of warming by 2100. The aim of this project is to investigate the if climate change has an impact on volatility within the stock market, and if so, how?

I will begin my research by focusing on the US due to data availibility and lengths of time series available.

Second-order ramifications of climate change are likely to be what impacts market volatility. Thus, I downloaded extreme weather data for analysis.

To determine causality I will t-test changes in volatility. I will use non-US stock indicies as control variable and US stock indicies as test variables.

I regressed the number of us climate disasters on mean global temperature anomoly to check correlation.

I developed a machine learning model with SKLearn in python to regress climate events on volatility. This showed there to be no correlation between volatility and climate change. SEE CODE.

Due to limited volatility indicies for global markets, I created my own measure (Wills Volatility). I produced a chart with a Loess regression of Wills Volatility against VIX to show correlation, and thus suitability (0.806 Pearsons Correlation Coefficient). SEE CODE.

Aligning market volatility time series with the dates that hurricanes hit was challenging. I decided to programme an algorithm that will automate the entire process for me, so by typing in the yahoo finance ticker of the desired stock/index, it returns an aligned dataframe of hurricane category, stock volatility, 21 point moving average volatility, 2 week trailing 21 point moving average of volatility (to account for weather forecasting). see code.

Since no correlation was found over long-term time frame. I will investigate effects on a short time frame around when severe storms hit since severe storms and tropical cyclones appear to have the largest correlation with global mean temp anomolies I will focus on looking at hurricanes.
